Drag Gambit — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 22, 2026

Summary: Drag Gambit does not collect any personal data that could identify you. It uses an anonymous analytics tool (GameAnalytics) to understand how the game is played and improve it. It can send you notifications about what's new, but only if you opt in, and you can turn them off at any time. There are no ads, no accounts, and we never sell or share data for commercial purposes.

What data we collect

The game uses GameAnalytics (GameAnalytics ApS, Denmark), a standard analytics tool for video games, which anonymously collects:

  • A random install identifier (it is not linked to your identity and changes if you reinstall the app).
  • Technical device data: model, Android version, language, and approximate country.
  • Gameplay events: session start and end, matches started and their outcome (win, loss, draw, or quit), and the difficulty level.

This data cannot identify you: we do not collect your name, email, contacts, precise location, or any advertising identifier. There are no accounts and no sign-up.

What we use it for

Only to improve the game: knowing how many people play, whether matches are finished or abandoned, and whether the difficulty is well tuned. Nothing else. There are no ads and no user profiles.

You can read GameAnalytics' privacy policy at gameanalytics.com/privacy.

Push notifications (optional)

The game can notify you about what's new (new queens, challenges, or game modes) through Firebase Cloud Messaging, a Google service. It is entirely optional: it only activates if you accept it inside the game and then grant Android's notification permission, and you can turn it off at any time from your device's notification settings.

To deliver notifications, Firebase generates a device registration token: a random identifier that is not linked to your identity and is only used to route the notification. Notifications are sent to broad topics (for example, the game's language), never targeted at individuals, and declining them changes nothing in the game.

You can read how Google handles this data at firebase.google.com/support/privacy.

Data stored on your device

Your game progress (card collection, team, and settings) is saved only in the app's private storage on your device. It never leaves it, and it is fully removed when you uninstall the app.

Permissions

The app uses the internet permission (to send the anonymous analytics and receive the notifications described above), the vibration permission, for haptic feedback when capturing cards, and the notifications permission, which is optional: it is only requested if you opt in to updates, and you can decline or revoke it later without changing anything in the game. It requests no other permission.

Children

The analytics are equally anonymous for everyone: no data is collected that could identify the person playing, whether a child or an adult.

Changes

If this policy ever changes (for example, if an online mode is added), this page will be updated before that version is released.
