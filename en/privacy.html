Drag Gambit — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

Summary: Drag Gambit does not collect any personal data that could identify you. It uses an anonymous analytics tool (GameAnalytics) to understand how the game is played and improve it. There are no ads, no accounts, and we never sell or share data for commercial purposes.

What data we collect

The game uses GameAnalytics (GameAnalytics ApS, Denmark), a standard analytics tool for video games, which anonymously collects:

  • A random install identifier (it is not linked to your identity and changes if you reinstall the app).
  • Technical device data: model, Android version, language, and approximate country.
  • Gameplay events: session start and end, matches started and their outcome (win, loss, draw, or quit), and the difficulty level.

This data cannot identify you: we do not collect your name, email, contacts, precise location, or any advertising identifier. There are no accounts and no sign-up.

What we use it for

Only to improve the game: knowing how many people play, whether matches are finished or abandoned, and whether the difficulty is well tuned. Nothing else. There are no ads and no user profiles.

You can read GameAnalytics' privacy policy at gameanalytics.com/privacy.

Data stored on your device

Your game progress (card collection, team, and settings) is saved only in the app's private storage on your device. It never leaves it, and it is fully removed when you uninstall the app.

Permissions

The app uses the internet permission (only to send the anonymous analytics described above) and the vibration permission, for haptic feedback when capturing cards. It requests no other permission.

Children

The analytics are equally anonymous for everyone: no data is collected that could identify the person playing, whether a child or an adult.

Changes

If this policy ever changes (for example, if an online mode is added), this page will be updated before that version is released.
